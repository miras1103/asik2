About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist. He developed the theory of relativity, which changed the way we understand space and time. His famous equation E = mc² showed the relationship between energy and mass. Einstein’s ideas deeply influenced modern physics and technology.

Achievements

  • Developed the theory of relativity, revolutionizing physics.
  • Created the famous equation E = mc², showing the link between mass and energy.
  • Won the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for explaining the photoelectric effect.
  • Contributed to quantum theory and the study of atomic energy.
  • Inspired many modern technologies, from nuclear power to GPS systems.

Important events

  1. 1879 – Born in Ulm, Germany.
  2. 1905 – Published the Annus Mirabilis papers, including special relativity.
  3. 1921 – Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for the photoelectric effect.
  4. 1933 – Emigrated to the United States, escaping Nazi Germany.
  5. 1939 – Signed a letter to Roosevelt that led to the Manhattan Project.
